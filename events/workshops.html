CNC

CNC stands for computer numerical control. A CNC router typically produces consistent and high-quality work and improves factory productivity. Unlike a jig router, the CNC router can produce a one-off as effectively as repeated identical production. Automation and precision are the key benefits of CNC router tables. A CNC router can reduce waste, frequency of errors, and the time the finished product takes to get to market.

Machining is about automating the machine tool operations for more precision and ease in manufacturing. In this project you will build a fully functional 3 axis CNC machine using Arduino which can perform manufacturing operations like drilling, milling and cutting. The tool will be operated with the help of G Codes and Arduino interfacing. By building this project, you will practically understand about CNC machining and work with stepper motors, Arduino board, G Codes, CNC calibration and programmin

Electrobotz

Electrobotz is a robotics workshop. This workshop is a combination of electronics and robotics. This two day workshop is designed for building autonomous robots (robots capable of making their own decision). Autonomous robots can make their own decisions with the help of a software program, but in this workshop we are using Hard-Wired programming, in which we can build an autonomous robot without using a microcontroller or a microprocessor. This workshop is not just robotics but also about electronics, for Hard-Wired programming, a very basic knowledge of electronics is required which will be taught in the course of the workshop. The participants are encouraged to build the different types of robots on their own with the help of the electronics knowledge obtained by them during the workshop. This will instigate the ideas in students for the next level of robotics, i.e. software programmed robots using a microcontroller.

This workshop will be conducted for 2 days (16 Hrs), with each day divided into 2 sessions. In this workshop, the different robots which will be built are as mentioned below

IR controlled robot

Obstacle Follower robot

Mobile controlled robot

Photophobic robot

Sound Controlled robot

Phototrophic robot

Line Following robot

Fire sensing robot

Obstacle Avoider robot

Build Your Dream Race Car

This workshop aims to provide a platform to understand the process through which a Race Car is conceived indulging in topics like Vehicle Dynamics and Fabrication techniques for the budding Engineers of tomorrow. The 2 day workshop includes activities which simulate the planning process required as well as give the students a firsthand experience of the challenge and difficulties they might encounter.

The first day of the workshop focuses on conception of a race car which primarily focus steps involved in understanding rules and regulations, setting targets, marking technical specifications, market survey, team management and delegation. And will delve deep into Elementary Vehicle Dynamics which primarily focus on Frame, Steering and Suspension and the day 1st ends with a group activity to give hands on experience about Business Plan .

The second day the workshop primarily deals with steps involved in fabrication and combustion engines. The 1st session deals with Jig setup, profiling of members, welding: types and steps involved. The pre-lunch session deals with concepts of ergonomics, Aerodynamics and types of brake systems. The 2nd session deals with Combustion Engines and will be concluded by a reason based Technical Quiz, a group activity.
