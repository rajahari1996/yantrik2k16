CAD WAR

Modelling a component is one of the essential step in any Design process which can be efficiently achieved with the help of computer often known as computer aided design This makes modelling simpler and makes products much quality full. Yantrik '16 paves a platform for the participants to involve in this event and help themselves to realise their inherent talents of drawing and modelling which may indirectly help the to pave a path for bright career.

Max no of participants: 1/ team

Round 1:
In this round all the participants have to attempt a written exam in which 10 questions has to be answered with in ten minutes. People shall be shortlisted after the 1st round

Round 2:
In this round, 3-D figures will be given to the participants and they have to finish the drawing within the prescribed time limit on the provided drawing sheets.

Note:

• During this event participant has to bring their own stationary
• Participants has to follow ASME Y14.5 Standards of dimensioning and tolerances

Round 3:
In this round participant will be given 3-D figures and part drawing. 3-D figure may also include assembly.
The participants will be given time limit for completion of the task given.

RULES:
1) Only ONE member is allowed to compete.
2) Models can be designed in any CAD software (CATIA/Pro- E/AutoCAD/Solidworks/Solid edge) and the final file must be prepared in IGES or STEP format or para-solid formats.
3) Those Participants who want to use software’s other than SOLID WORKS AND AUTO CAD should bring their own laptop with pre-installed software. Competitors have to show different views from a given isometric figure or isometric design during the evaluation.
4) Design should be completed within specified time.
5) Use of Internet or any electronic gadget is restricted. If found, it will lead to disqualification. Design will be judged on the basis of accuracy, completeness, and dimensions. Each Question will carry some points according to the type of question and its level of difficulty.

Judging:
Decision of Judges will be final and binding.
Any sign of plagiarism from Internet orhyfrom other participant will lead to direct disqualification.
Participant can only submit once. In case of multiple submissions only the first entry will be considered for evaluation.

The design will be evaluated on the following aspects:
1) Originality of the Design
2) Clear and easily comprehensible design
3) Time Taken

No claim will be entertained against the announced results.

AQUA ROCKET

When you launch a rocket, you’re not really flying that rocket. You’re just sort of hanging on. – Michael P. Anderson

YANTRIK`16 brings forth a platform to make your own rocket! Larger the distance a rocket covers, the better it is. But the challenge is not limited to maximum range but accuracy as well. Put on your thinking caps and hatch out a new design that stands apart from the rest and fulfills your dream of flight! This is certainly a test on the aerodynamics of model. And most importantly, it`s just fun to make a rocket.

ROUND 1(RANGE): In this round, the teams will be tested for the maximum horizontal distance travelled by their rocket.
The rocket has to be launched such that it covers a minimum distance of 35 meters.
The teams which cross 35 meters will be qualified for the second round.
Scores will be awarded as per the given formula
Score = Total distance travelled in met

ROUND 2(ACCURACY):
The rocket should be launched from a distance from pre-selected target and depending upon the deviation from the bull’s eye, and one ring will arrange in front of target all participants should launch the rocket through that ring ,scores will awarded(Maximum 100 points if it hits bull’s eye).
Finally, for results, sum of the scores from both rounds will be taken into consideration.
In case of tie, 3rd round will be conducted.

ROUND 3(TIME OF FLIGHT):
The team with maximum time of flight will win tie breaker (or) winner will be decided on launcher design

Rules and regulations:
• Team should consist a maximum of 3 members.
• The water will be provided on the venue.
• No additives should be used.
• Soft drinks/Aerated water are not allowed
• Chemical propellants and boosters are strictly prohibited.
• Participants should bring their own equipment i.e., launcher, bottles, foot pumps(pressure pump), etc.
• Two chances will be given to each team and best of two will be considered.
• Pressure inside the bottle should not exceed 90 psi in both rounds.
• The launcher angle is of team’s choice.

NOTE: Any team will be disqualified:
• If rocket explodes before the launch.
• If any rocket is found dangerous for launch by the organizer and judges.
• If any readymade models are used.
• The organizer reserves all rights to change any of the above rules as they dream fit. Changes in rules if any, will be highlighted on the website. In case of any discrepancy, the decision of judges shall be treated as final.

NAVIKA

Navika is a competition aimed towards creating an interest among the engineering students towards the new concept of designing a water vehicle. Design a water vehicle (ship or boat) as per your interest by following the specification, make a model and compete with the opponents in the given arena.
Max. number of participants :3/ team

Rules and regulations:
1. The Arena dimensions will be given on spot.
2. Participants should bring DC motor, propeller, battery, wires (any other material if they want they can).
3. Design of your vehicle should be finished before the starting of the event.
4. Design should be as per specifications.
5. If reason is genuine, we will try to provide material for repairs.
6. No physical force is applied while racing, if any team do this type of activities that team will be eliminated.

Specifications:
1. Dimensions of 30*15 (l*b).
2.The maximum limit of battery is 12V.
3. The motor speed limit is 300rpm.

Round-1 (Design analysis and race for single team only):
Design of the water boat will be checked by following specification limits. Then only the boat will be permitted to enter the arena for race. The teams whose vehicles will balance and move without sinking will be qualified for next round. Time will be considered for the participants to send them to second round.

Round-2 (race between two teams):
All the qualified teams will be participating in the race with their opponents (i.e. is decided by the coordinators of the event), obstacles are arranged in the arena. Best of them will be declared by considering the progress of the team, model, some features like aerodynamic shape, size, weight, materials used etc.

Note: Time will be taken to consideration to declare the winner.

ROBO RACE & WAR

Now a-days everyone like racing but on plane roads this year’s Robo race gives you an opportunity to build an all-terrain rover to test you’re off road skills. In this competition you are required to build a rover that fulfills the specifications given below and traverse an impassable terrain that would be created at the event location.

max number of participants :3/ team

Design specifications:

1) The device must be powered by rechargeable batteries. No other sources of energy are permitted.

2) The device must be controlled either through a transmitter/receiver radio link or through an umbilical cord. An umbilical cord controller may not contain any batteries.

3) The transmitter/receiver radio link may be any commercially available model controller

4) All radio controllers will be impounded and shut off at the competition, except during the team’s run

5) Mechanical forces on the umbilical cord may not be used to help propel or control the vehicle. The umbilical cord must be detachable from the vehicle using a commercial connector. The umbilical card may not be a part of the supporting structure of the device. The umbilical cord may only be used to transmit the commands from the controller. Teams will be disqualified if, at any time, the umbilical cord is used to apply mechanical force to the device.

6) Robot dimensions should not exceed 25*25 (L*B) (in cm)(with wheels).

7) The battery voltage should not exceed more than 12 volts.

EVENT DETAILS:

The event is a team event with at least 2 members but not more than 4 members per team.

RACING REGION:

Racing region is the uneven terrain as the round number increases intensity of obstacles increase depending up on the number of teams.

In this round the participant is given a maze to solve and he needs to find his way out of that maze and make his rover to pass through the track. Total time is considered for the bot to pass the track.
